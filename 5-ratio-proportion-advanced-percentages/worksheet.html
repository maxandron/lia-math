1. Ratio Word Problems
Read each problem carefully. Identify what you know and what you need to find. Set up a proportion and solve for the unknown value. Show all your working.
1.
It takes 16g of butter to make 27 croissants. How much butter do you need to make 8 croissants? Give your answer to 1 decimal place.
2.
A recipe for 4 people requires 300g of flour. How much flour is needed for 10 people?
3.
A car travels 135 miles using 9 litres of petrol. How many litres would be needed to travel 200 miles? Give your answer to 1 decimal place.
4.
To make concrete, sand and cement are mixed in the ratio 5:2. If I use 15kg of sand, how much cement do I need?
5.
A photograph measuring 6cm by 9cm is enlarged so that the shorter side is now 10cm. What is the length of the longer side?
6.
5 pencils cost £1.80. How much would 8 pencils cost?
7.
A map has a scale of 1:50,000. Two towns are 8cm apart on the map. What is the actual distance between them in kilometres?
8.
Orange juice is made by mixing concentrate and water in the ratio 1:4. If I want to make 750ml of orange juice, how much concentrate do I need?
9.
Three workers can paint a fence in 12 hours. How long would it take 5 workers, working at the same rate?
10.
Gold is mixed with copper in the ratio 7:3 to make jewellery. If a necklace weighs 45g, how much pure gold does it contain?
2. Percentage Word Problems
Read each problem carefully. Identify whether you need to find a percentage of an amount, increase/decrease by a percentage, or work backwards from a final amount. Show all your working.
1.
A $450 Prada bag goes on sale for 20% off. What is the price of the bag after the sale?
2.
A laptop costs £680 before VAT. VAT is added at 20%. What is the total price including VAT?
3.
A restaurant bill comes to £84 before service charge. A 12.5% service charge is added. What is the total amount to pay?
4.
A shop increases the price of trainers from £60 to £75. What is the percentage increase?
5.
After a 15% discount, a jacket costs £76.50. What was the original price?
6.
In a test, Sarah scored 42 out of 60. What is her percentage score?
7.
A car was bought for £15,000 and sold one year later for £12,750. Calculate the percentage loss.
8.
A mobile phone plan costs £25 per month. The company announces a 8% price increase. What will the new monthly cost be?
9.
In a sale, a television is reduced by 35%. The sale price is £390. What was the original price?
10.
A savings account contains £2,400. Interest is paid at 3.5% per year. How much interest is earned after one year?
