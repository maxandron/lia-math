Rounding Help

1. Rounding to Nearest 10/100/1000

Rule: Look at the digit to the right of the place you're rounding to. If it's 5 or more, round up. If it's less than 5, round down.

Example: Round 347 to the nearest ten

• Look at the ones digit: 7
• 7 ≥ 5, so round up
• Answer: 350

2. Rounding to Decimal Places

Rule: Count the decimal places you need, then look at the next digit. If it's 5 or more, round up the last digit. Otherwise, keep it.

Example: Round 3.4567 to 2 decimal places

• Keep first 2 decimals: 3.45
• Look at next digit: 6
• 6 ≥ 5, so round up
• Answer: 3.46

3. Rounding to Significant Figures

Rule: Count from the first non-zero digit. Keep that many digits, then look at the next digit to decide if you round up or down.

Example: Round 4567 to 2 significant figures

• First 2 significant digits: 45
• Look at next digit: 6
• 6 ≥ 5, so round up
• Answer: 4600
